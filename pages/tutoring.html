Office hours

Office hours are where Ada's Team volunteers will sit in the room and make sure it is open so YOU can use it! You can:

  • Recharge in comfortable environment
  • Meet new people who care about diversity in our department
  • Visit and hangout
  • Have a cup of tea
  • Read a zine or book (from our tiny Library)
  • Plan a diversity focused event
  • Please note that the office will be unavailable for general hangouts during any official tutoring sessions being held in the office.

Tutoring

Looking for a tutor? Ada’s Team has some members who are willing to help! We are committed to providing a safe space for students, so if you have any particular tutoring requirements, send us an email at adasteam@ualberta.ca We will do our best to accommodate. Volunteer tutors can help you…

  • Understand computing related concepts
  • Develop debugging skills
  • Apply good coding practices

Please keep the following in mind:

  • Tutoring hours are run by volunteers
  • Tutoring hours are drop–in
  • It is not reasonable to expect that volunteer tutors will look over your code ahead of their tutoring hour

Click on tutoring sessions to see what course(s) help will be provided for. Volunteer tutors provide help in Ada’s Base, CSC 1-24. There is a map of CSC in the "Find Us" tab if you do not know where that is! If you would like to become an Ada's Team tutor send us an email at adasteam@ualberta.ca

When you're done with a tutoring session, please fill out the tutoring feedback form!
